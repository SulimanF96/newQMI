Tenormin contains a medicine called atenolol. This belongs to a group of medicines called beta-blockers. \n \nTenormin is used to:\n\n • Treat high blood pressure (hypertension). \n \n • Treat uneven heart beats (arrhythmias).\n \n • Help prevent chest pain (angina). \n \n • Protect the heart in the early treatment after a\nheart attack (myocardial infarction). \n\nIt works by making your heart beat more slowly and with less force.

Contradictions

Do not take Tenormin if:\n \n-You are allergic (hypersensitive) to atenolol or\nany of the other ingredients of Tenormin 100 mg Tablets .\n\n- You have ever had any of the following heart problems:\n\n -heart failure which is not under control (this usually makes you breathless and causes your ankles to swell). \n \n -second- or third-degree heart block (a condition which may be treated by a pacemaker) very slow or very uneven heart beats, very low blood pressure or very poor circulation.\n \n-You have a tumour called phaeochromocytoma that is not being treated. This is usually near your\nhey and can cause high blood pressure. If you are being treated for phaeochromocytoma, your doctor will give you another medicine, called an alpha-blocker, to take as well as Tenormin. \n\n- You have been told that you have higher than normal levels of acid in your blood (metabolic\nacidosis). \n\nCheck with your doctor or pharmacist before taking Tenormin if: \n\n- You have asthma, wheezing or any other\nsimilar breathing problems, or you get allergic reactions, for example to insect stings. If you have ever had asthma or wheezing, do not take this medicine without first checking with your doctor.\n\n- You have a type of chest pain (angina) called Prinzmetal's angina. \n\n- You have poor blood circulation or controlled heart failure.\n\n- You have first-degree heart block. \n\n- You have diabetes. Your medicine may change\nhow you respond to having low blood sugar. You may feel your heart beating faster. \n\n-You have thyrotoxicosis (a condition caused by overactive thyroid gland). Your medicine may hide the symptoms of thyrotoxicosis. \n\n-You have problems with your kidneys. You may need to have some check-ups during your treatment.

Side Effects

Allergic reactions:\n \n If you have an allergic reaction, see a doctor straight away. The signs may include raised lumps on your skin (weals), or swelling of your face, lips, mouth, tongue or throat \n \nOther possible side effects: Common (affects less than 1 in 10 people)\n\n-You may notice that your pulse rate becomes slower while you are taking the tablets. This is normal, but if you are concerned please tell your doctor about it. \n\n-Cold hands and feet.\n-Diarrhoea. \n- Feeling sick (nausea).\n- Feeling tired. \n\nUncommon (affects less than 1 in 100 people)\n\n- Disturbed sleep. \n\nRare (affects less than 1 in 1,000 people)\n\n - Heart block (which can cause dizziness, abnormal\nheart beat, tiredness or fainting).\n- Numbness and spasm in your fingers which is\nfollowed by warmth and pain (Raynaud's disease).\n- Mood changes.\n-Nightmares. \n-Feeling confused.\n- Changes in personality (psychoses) or hallucinations\n-Headache.\n- Dizziness (particularly when standing up). \n- Tingling of your hands.

Storage

Keep your medicine in a safe place where children cannot see or reach it. Your medicine could harm them.\n- Do not store above 25°C. Store your tablets in the original package. Keep the blister strip in the carton. This will protect your medicine from light and moisture. \n- Do not use your tablets after the expiry date which is stated on the blister strip and carton. The\nexpiry date refers to the last day of that month. \n\nMedicines should not be disposed of via wastewater or household waste. Ask your pharmacist how to dispose of medicines that are no longer required. These measures will help to protect the environment.

Components

\nThe active substance is atenolol. Each tablet contains 100 mg (milligrams) of atenolol. The other ingredients are gelatin, magnesium carbonate, magnesium stearate, methylhydroxypropylcellulose, sodium laurilsulfate, maize starch, titanium dioxide (E171), Macrogol, Sunset Yellow Lake (E110) and talc.
